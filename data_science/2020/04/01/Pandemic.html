SIR model

From Wikipedia: SIR.

The SIR model without vital dynamics:The dynamics of an epidemic, for example the flu, > are often much faster than the dynamics of birth and death, therefore, birth and death are often omitted in simple compartmental models. The SIR system without so-called vital dynamics (birth and death, sometimes called demography) described above can be expressed by the following set of ordinary differential equations:$$\begin{aligned} &{\frac {dS}{dt}}=-{\frac {\beta IS}{N}},\\ &{\frac {dI}{dt}}={\frac {\beta IS}{N}}-\gamma I,\\ &{\frac {dR}{dt}}=\gamma I, \end{aligned} $$

At time $t$ the SIR model is characterised by $S(t)$ for the number of susceptible in the population, $I(t)$ for the number of infected, and $R(t)$ for the number of recovered or deceased (or immune) individuals out of the population $N = S(t)+I(t)+R(t)$.
